Meet
Jerry

Jerry Letegio isn't your typical travel blogger. He's someone who figured out the hard way that waiting for the "perfect time" means never going at all. The trips planned on a tight budget, the ones that almost didn't happen — those are the ones that mattered most.

This guide is built from real experience: booking flights last minute, navigating unfamiliar towns, stretching budgets across weekends, and coming back with stories worth telling. No sponsorships, no fluff — just practical wisdom for people who actually want to get out there.

Advice

ESCAPE
Tips

Don't be afraid to get away when you've got a good plan. Here are Jerry's tips for making every trip run smoothly.

01
🛂

Plan ahead

Don't wait until the last minute. Trips are way more enjoyable when you're prepared — no panicking, no rushing, just smooth sailing from start to finish.

02
✈️

Wake up early for long trips

Make sure you're ready before you head out — eat a proper meal, finish packing, and avoid the morning rush. A good start makes the whole trip better.

03
💰

Save smart

Learn how to budget effectively before you go. Set a spending limit so you don't run out of cash mid-trip — and so there's still something left over for pasalubong.

04
🌐

Research before you commit

As someone who loves to travel, you need to know where you're going and how much it'll cost. Don't show up blind — the more you know, the more you enjoy.

05
🏨

Pick the right place to stay

Don't just crash at a friend's place — you might end up more tired than when you left. Book your own spot so you actually get to rest and recharge properly.

06
🤫

Don't overshare your plans

The more people who know about your trip, the higher the chance it doesn't happen. Keep it simple — only tell the people who are actually coming with you.

07
💵

Never forget to bring money

Always have cash on you when you're out. No money means no fun — you can't eat what you want, you can't buy what you want, and the whole vibe falls apart.

08
📱

Download offline maps before you leave

Save Google Maps offline so you won't get lost even without signal. Grab a translation app too — it always comes in handy in places with a different language.

Before You Leave

Trip
Checklist

Your Progress 0 / 24 packed

📋 Documents

  • Valid ID (at least 6 months before expiry)
  • Visa, if required at your destination
  • Travel insurance for the unexpected
  • Hotel and transportation confirmation
  • Copies of all documents — on your phone and printed
  • Emergency contact list

💼 What to Pack

  • Clothes for three days — just do laundry on the go
  • Universal travel adapter
  • Power bank, at least 10,000mAh
  • Debit or credit card with no foreign transaction fee
  • Cash in the local currency of your destination
  • Basic first aid kit and any personal medication

📱 Essential Apps

  • Google Maps — download offline before you leave
  • Google Translate for language barriers
  • XE Currency Converter for money exchange
  • Booking.com or Agoda for accommodations
  • Grab or Gojek for getting around the city
  • Skyscanner to find the cheapest flights

🔐 Safety Tips

  • Let someone trusted know where you're going
  • Don't show off how much money you have
  • Lock your valuables in a locker or safe spot
  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings
  • Look up the emergency number of your destination
  • Always have a backup way to communicate
